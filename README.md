# Towards a Cashless Society in Bahrain

A research project exploring digital payment adoption and public readiness in the Kingdom of Bahrain.

This project investigates the transition towards a cashless or less-cash society in Bahrain by examining citizens' and residents' willingness to adopt digital payment systems. The research focuses on understanding the factors that influence adoption, identifying barriers, and providing recommendations for a secure and inclusive digital financial ecosystem.

## ğŸ” Key Findings

The study surveyed 161 participants in Bahrain and found:

- **Social Influence** emerged as the strongest predictor (Î² = 0.247) of cashless payment adoption
- Five key factors significantly predict usage: self-efficacy, trust, social influence, perceived benefit, and ease of use
- 57.1% of participants do not carry cash regularly
- The regression model achieved exceptional fit (RÂ² = 0.990), explaining 99% of variance in cashless payment usage
- Despite awareness of security concerns, these did not significantly deter adoption among the primarily young, educated sample
